sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: When testing the "reversed50000.txt", it takes more time than the other two options. Because of the way bubble sort works a reversed array is the worst case scenario, and that's why it takes more time than the other two programs.

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: When testing with "random50000.txt", it gives the faster time. The other two programs give similar times.

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: If tested with the "sorted50000.txt", it takes more time than the other two sorting programs. Selection sort has to make sure the number is in the right place; even if they are already sorted.
